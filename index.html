From Pixels to Perfection: The Evolution of First-Person Shooters

First-person shooters have come a long way since the pixelated corridors of Wolfenstein 3D. What began as simple maze-like levels and basic gunplay has evolved into a genre that defines modern gaming. From the revolutionary multiplayer arenas of Quake to the cinematic campaigns of Call of Duty, and the tactical precision of Counter-Strike to the hero-based chaos of Overwatch, FPS games have continuously pushed the boundaries of technology, storytelling, and competitive play. Today, they stand at the intersection of esports, virtual reality, and cutting-edge graphics, offering experiences that would have seemed impossible just decades ago. This is the story of how we went from shooting demons in hallways to commanding entire battlefields with friends across the globe.
